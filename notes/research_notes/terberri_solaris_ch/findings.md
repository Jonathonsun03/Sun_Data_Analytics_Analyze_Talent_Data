## Qualitative Themes

### 1) Stable, host-led interaction rhythm
The channel summaries repeatedly describe a streamer-led format where chat is acknowledged but the stream structure remains primarily controlled by the host. Interaction is present but generally less interruptive and less bursty.

### 2) Slow-tempo community ritual
With `80/82` streams marked as slow pacing, the dominant social mode is steady and ongoing rather than event-spike driven. The language in summaries points to consistent hosting ritual rather than campaign-like momentum.

### 3) Serialization over eventization
The strongest pattern is long-form progression arcs (especially across recurring game series), indicating audience retention through continuity and installment structure rather than one-off spectacles.

### 4) Monetization is analytically absent in this corpus
No summaries in this folder contain visible paid-message inventories. As a result, monetary-role conclusions are mostly constrained to template language and cannot be empirically expanded from this subset alone.

### 5) Relationship framing emphasizes continuity
Even when reciprocity labels vary, the relationship model statement is highly consistent. This suggests a persistent identity: dependable host role, communal tone-setting by chat, and stable social expectations over time.

## Implications
- Programming strength appears to be consistency and serialized follow-through.
- Engagement appears less dependent on donation-triggered spikes.
- If experimentation is desired, this corpus leaves room to test more eventized formats without disrupting core identity.

## Caveats
- Summaries are second-order interpretations, not raw transcript re-coding.
- Monetary conclusions are limited by complete paid-message absence in this folder.
- Relationship-model uniformity may partly reflect template bias in summary generation.
