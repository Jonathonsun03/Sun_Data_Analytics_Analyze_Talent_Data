## Qualitative Themes

### 1) High-reciprocity co-performance is the norm
This corpus is strongly chat-responsive. Summaries consistently describe frequent chat acknowledgments, call-and-response rhythms, and active integration of viewer prompts into stream flow.

### 2) Monetization operates as social ritual, not only transaction
Paid messages are common and often embedded in celebratory or playful moments. Donations appear to function as public participation signals (support + recognition + momentum), rather than isolated financial events.

### 3) Eventized engagement architecture
Compared with purely steady streams, these summaries show repeated “moment clustering” around goals, milestones, challenge escalations, and themed stream arcs. This creates periodic intensity spikes that reinforce community attention.

### 4) Persona-led intimacy scaffolds participation
The summaries frequently describe warm, humorous, and self-disclosing host behavior. This appears to support both emotional closeness and high chat throughput, while preserving clear host leadership.

### 5) High engagement can exist without paid messages
A meaningful subset of streams shows high reciprocity even with no visible paid messages, indicating that monetary activity amplifies a pre-existing social engine rather than replacing it.

## Implications
- Core channel strength is participatory momentum and emotional accessibility.
- Donation mechanics appear most effective when attached to narrative goals or shared rituals.
- Repeatable high-performing formats likely include interactive art/chat, story-reaction arcs, and event streams.

## Caveats
- Some files use richer narrative templates while others use concise templates; both were normalized where possible.
- Summary text is interpretive and may carry template bias.
- “Visible paid-message inventory” reflects what appears in summaries, not necessarily full platform-level payment logs.
